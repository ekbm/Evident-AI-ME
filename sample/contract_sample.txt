SERVICE AGREEMENT

This Service Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of January 1, 2025, by and between:

PROVIDER: TechCorp Solutions Inc., a Delaware corporation ("Provider")
CLIENT: Acme Industries Ltd., a California corporation ("Client")

1. SERVICES
Provider agrees to deliver cloud hosting and maintenance services for Client's web applications.

2. PAYMENT TERMS
Client shall pay Provider the sum of $5,000 USD monthly for the services rendered.
Payment is due within 14 days of receiving each monthly invoice.
Late payments shall incur a 1.5% monthly interest charge.

3. TERM AND TERMINATION
This Agreement shall commence on January 1, 2025, and continue for a period of 12 months.
Either party may terminate this Agreement with 30 days written notice.
Upon termination, Client must pay all outstanding invoices within 7 days.

4. OBLIGATIONS OF PROVIDER
Provider must maintain 99.9% uptime for all hosted services.
Provider shall respond to critical support tickets within 2 hours.
Provider must perform weekly backups of all Client data.
Provider shall provide monthly performance reports by the 5th of each month.

5. OBLIGATIONS OF CLIENT
Client must provide access credentials to Provider within 5 business days of signing.
Client shall notify Provider of any security concerns immediately.
Client must not share Provider's proprietary tools with third parties.
Client shall maintain current contact information for emergency notifications.

6. CONFIDENTIALITY
Both parties agree to keep confidential all proprietary information disclosed during the term.
Confidential information shall not be disclosed to third parties without written consent.
This confidentiality obligation survives termination for 3 years.

7. LIABILITY
Provider's total liability shall not exceed the fees paid in the preceding 12 months.
Neither party shall be liable for indirect, incidental, or consequential damages.

8. DISPUTE RESOLUTION
Any disputes shall be resolved through binding arbitration in San Francisco, California.
The prevailing party shall be entitled to recover reasonable attorney's fees.
